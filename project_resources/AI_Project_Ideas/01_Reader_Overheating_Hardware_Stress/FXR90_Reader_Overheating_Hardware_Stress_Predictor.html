01 / Executive Summary
Project Summary

Predict reader failure before operations are affected

Fixed RFID readers often run continuously in warehouses, portals, docks, plants, and retail environments. Over time, heat, CPU load, memory pressure, storage pressure, radio instability, and power issues can reduce reliability. This project creates a predictive maintenance layer that watches every FXR90 reader and identifies risk before it becomes downtime.

Thermal Detect rising ambient and PA temperature patterns.
Compute Track CPU, RAM, app load, and uptime stress.
Storage Predict flash and log saturation before failure.
Stability Connect warnings, errors, radio state, and power health.

Business Problem

  • Readers can fail silently until tag reads drop or stop.
  • Overheating can cause unstable radio performance and reboots.
  • High CPU, RAM, or full flash can impact data delivery.
  • Maintenance teams need early signals, not only after-failure logs.

Proposed AI Outcome

  • Reader stress score for each device.
  • Low, Medium, High, Critical risk labels.
  • Predicted reason: heat, CPU/RAM, storage, radio, power, or app stress.
  • Recommended action for operations and maintenance teams.

Showcase Statement

The project converts raw FXR90 operational telemetry into an AI-powered reader health monitor that predicts overheating and hardware stress, helping teams prevent RFID downtime and reduce reactive maintenance.

Source signals reviewed from docs/openapi_md.json: get_status, heartbeat, error, warning, logs, firmware, network, and management events.

Important Scope

This project predicts the health of the RFID reader system itself. Predictive maintenance for external machines such as motors, pumps, or conveyors would require additional machine sensor data such as vibration, current, temperature, PLC faults, and runtime.

Prediction Design
03 / Stress Score
AI Logic

Convert telemetry into a continuous hardware stress score

The MVP should use a hybrid approach: business rules for immediate value, baseline anomaly detection for each reader, and later supervised ML when enough historical failures and maintenance actions are available.

Risk Components

Temperature
35%
CPU/RAM
25%
Flash
15%
Error Rate
15%
Power/Radio
10%
ReaderStressScore =
0.35 * temperature_risk
+ 0.25 * compute_memory_risk
+ 0.15 * flash_storage_risk
+ 0.15 * warning_error_risk
+ 0.10 * power_radio_risk

Risk Level:
0-39 Low | 40-64 Medium
65-84 High | 85-100 Critical

Rule Layer

  • Immediate alerts for disconnected radio, power negotiation failure, very high temperature, or flash nearly full.
  • Useful from day one, even before training data exists.

Baseline Layer

  • Build a normal profile per reader by hour, location, and workload.
  • Detect abnormal temperature rise or CPU usage compared with that reader's own history.

ML Layer

  • Train on historical warnings, errors, reboots, service tickets, and maintenance outcomes.
  • Predict risk in the next 1 hour, 6 hours, and 24 hours.
Example Indicator Detection Logic Suggested Action
PA temperature rising quickly Temperature slope above reader baseline for 15-30 minutes. Check ventilation, reduce RF duty cycle, inspect installation location.
High CPU and warning growth CPU above threshold plus increasing numWarnings. Inspect reader gateway workload, user apps, and data batching.
Memory pressure RAM used trending upward with no return to baseline. Check for long-running app issue or schedule controlled restart.
Flash saturation Reader data or root filesystem used percentage approaches high-risk threshold. Clear logs, reduce logging level, retrieve and rotate diagnostic archives.

Value and Conclusion
07 / Showcase Close
Business Value

Why this is a strong AI project for the company

This project is practical, data-ready, and directly tied to operations. It uses existing FXR90 API data to prevent downtime, improve maintenance planning, and give teams a concrete AI use case that can be demonstrated quickly.

Less Downtime Catch stress before readers fail or reboot unexpectedly.
Faster RCA Explain whether risk is thermal, compute, storage, radio, power, or app-related.
Smarter Work Prioritize maintenance by risk instead of checking every reader equally.

KPIs to Track

  • Reader downtime hours per month.
  • Number of surprise reader failures.
  • Mean time to detect hardware stress.
  • Mean time to resolve reader incidents.
  • False positive and false negative alert rate.
  • Percentage of alerts with completed maintenance action.

Stakeholder Pitch

We can use the FXR90's own heartbeat and diagnostic events to predict overheating and hardware stress before RFID operations degrade. The MVP can start with explainable scoring and anomaly detection, then evolve into a trained failure prediction model as maintenance history grows.

Recommended Next Step

Build a small proof of concept using 3-5 readers. Collect heartbeat and status telemetry for at least two weeks, implement the stress score, validate alert quality with operations, and then expand to all production readers.
